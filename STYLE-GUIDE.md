POCKET PARAMOUR — VISUAL STYLE GUIDE
=====================================

Audience: the owner (drawing the art) and any future illustrator.
Purpose: keep the seven characters feeling like they're in the same
game even though they're in seven different emotional registers.

The single most important rule: **a player who switches between
two character cards should immediately feel they're in the same
world.** Not the same outfit. Not the same pose. The same SOFTNESS,
the same line-weight, the same render-language for skin/hair/eyes.

If your sketches feel "off" but you can't tell why — it's almost
always one of the rules below being broken.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. UNIVERSAL RULES (apply to all 7 characters)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 1.1 Render style
Soft anime / Otome-VN style. NOT chibi. NOT photoreal.
- Line weight: thin and confident. Outer silhouette ~2px equivalent;
  internal detail lines ~1px. NO chunky outlines.
- Eyes: large, soft, with at least 2 highlights (catchlight + iris-
  glint). Lashes drawn separately from the eye outline (delicate,
  not thick).
- Skin: gradient shading, not flat. Soft cheek blush on every
  character regardless of gender or attitude. Even Noir.
- Hair: visible strand grouping. Each character has 3–5 hair "groups"
  that move together (forelock, side strand, etc.). NOT one solid
  blob.
- Mouth: small, expressive. Closed-mouth slight smile is the default.
  Open-mouth happy is reserved for high-affection moments.

### 1.2 The "dreamy" rule
**Every character has a dreamy quality.** The differences are in HOW
the dreaminess manifests. Read the voice-direction header in each
character file before drawing — it tells you the SPECIFIC dreaminess
you're rendering.

### 1.3 Backgrounds
- Soft, painterly, low-contrast.
- Each character has a SIGNATURE BACKGROUND palette (see § 3 below).
- Backgrounds never compete with the character. Character's eyes
  are always the brightest point in any portrait.

### 1.4 Lighting
- Always one warm soft key light + one cool soft fill.
- Specific to character (Alistair: candle/torch; Noir: violet bleed;
  Lyra: filtered cave light; etc.) — see § 3.
- Hard shadows: never. Even in dramatic scenes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. PER-CHARACTER PALETTE + RENDER NOTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Each entry: dominant palette · accent · skin/hair render notes ·
"dreamy" tell.

### ALISTAIR — The Dreamy Knight (Aragorn-warm)
- **Palette**: warm gold + steel + cream. Low saturation.
- **Accent**: torchlight orange in highlights.
- **Skin**: tan, warm, soft. A few faint scars (jaw, brow) — small,
  almost decorative. Stubble sometimes.
- **Hair**: dark blond / chestnut. Slightly tousled. Wavy. NOT shiny.
- **Eyes**: warm hazel/amber. The KINDEST eyes in the cast.
- **Dreamy tell**: he is always looking at the player slightly too
  long. The pupil is wide-set. There's a weight in the gaze that
  says "I have been guarding you in my head for weeks."
- **Armor render**: brushed steel, never mirror-bright. Leather and
  cloth visible underneath. Gauntlets unbuckled in soft moments.
- **Signature scenes** (must be drawable):
   - Standing at a gate, cloak around the player's shoulders
   - Forehead-press at the watchhouse
   - Bare hand offered, gauntlet on the table

### ELIAN — Dreamy-Quiet Warden (Jamie-Fraser-low)
- **Palette**: forest green + earth brown + moss. Muted.
- **Accent**: golden-hour sun-through-leaves dapple.
- **Skin**: sun-warm tan. Weather-worn but soft. Older eyes than the
  rest of the cast (he is centuries old, but you should NOT draw
  him as old — draw him as someone who has SEEN much).
- **Hair**: dark brown / black. Long-ish. Often tied back loosely
  with a leather cord. Some of it loose around the face.
- **Eyes**: deep green. Not bright — patient.
- **Dreamy tell**: gaze is steady but slightly unfocused, as if he
  is always also listening to the forest. Eyebrows slightly low,
  not in a frown — in attention.
- **Clothing**: layered, organic. Cloak with hood pushed back.
  Forest leather. Never armor. Always practical.
- **Signature scenes**:
   - Standing under a rowan with the player
   - Cloak drape around the player's shoulders, looking at the fire
   - Wordless hand-take at the creek

### LYRA — Dreamy Melancholy Siren (Florence-Welch + Galadriel)
- **Palette**: deep blue + teal + pearl. Cool.
- **Accent**: bioluminescent cyan in cave-light scenes; salt-pink
  in sea-scenes.
- **Skin**: pale, slightly cool-toned. **Subtle pearlescent shimmer
  on the cheekbones, neck, and collarbone** — she is half-siren.
  Never overdo this; it should look like skin that ALMOST has
  scales but doesn't.
- **Hair**: long, silver-white-with-blue undertone, or dark with
  silver strands. ALWAYS slightly windblown / wet-looking. The
  hair is half-alive.
- **Eyes**: pale blue or grey-green. Slightly larger than realistic.
  Look like sea-glass.
- **Dreamy tell**: she's always slightly half-smiling, like she just
  finished singing something or is about to. Her lips are slightly
  parted in idle.
- **Clothing**: layered, draped, sea-shell + bone + freshwater pearl.
  Never armored. Bare arms or thin sleeves. Her mother's STAFF is
  always near her — pale wood, runed.
- **Signature scenes**:
   - Hand-taking the player at the cave entrance, cold fingers
     finding warm
   - Sitting by the tide, hair lit from one side
   - Singing into a cup of seawater (no, seriously — that's a beat)

### CASPIAN — Dreamy Charming Prince (Richard Madden + Matt Smith)
- **Palette**: warm rose-gold + deep wine + cream. Royal but warm.
- **Accent**: candlelight glow on skin; gold thread on clothing.
- **Skin**: golden-warm, healthy, soft. The MOST conventionally
  beautiful character in the cast (this is intentional — he was
  made to be looked at).
- **Hair**: dark brown / chestnut, wavy, slightly artful. Always
  looks intentional even when "messy."
- **Eyes**: warm amber-brown. Crinkles when he smiles. Tired
  underneath when caught off-guard.
- **Dreamy tell**: HALF-distracted. He's always looking at the
  player like he's just thought of something he can't quite remember
  but it has to do with them. The dreamy is in the SOFT FOCUS of
  his attention.
- **Clothing**: court formal, but the top button is always undone
  in private. Velvet, silk, embroidery. NEVER armor. The crown
  appears in some portraits but not all — when it's off, that's
  meaningful.
- **Signature scenes**:
   - The hand-kiss that lingers (held longer than propriety allows)
   - Forehead against yours on the balcony
   - Pouring tea for two in a private room

### LUCIEN — Cool Intellectual Scholar (Cumberbatch + Chalamet)
- **Palette**: cool violet + dusk-purple + ink-black. Cerebral.
- **Accent**: candlelight gold on skin/hair (the warm note in the
  cool palette — important). Faint ink-stains on fingertips.
- **Skin**: pale, cool, soft. Slightly tired-around-the-eyes (he
  forgets to sleep). Sometimes a faint smudge of ink on his face
  he hasn't noticed.
- **Hair**: dark brown / near-black, wavy or curly. Often slightly
  unkempt — he does not fuss. Sometimes a strand falls in his
  face when he leans over a book.
- **Eyes**: deep blue, very intelligent. Slightly squinted (focus,
  not suspicion). Reading glasses in some portraits.
- **Dreamy tell**: he's always slightly off-center — looking at
  something just past the player, then realizing they're there
  and refocusing. The "lost in thought" gaze that snaps back into
  presence is HIS dreaminess.
- **Clothing**: scholarly. Layered robes / longcoat / waistcoat.
  Ink-stained sleeves. Always one piece slightly askew (a button,
  a collar). NEVER perfectly neat.
- **Signature scenes**:
   - Pen-down ritual: pen set with deliberate care, page turned
     facedown, looks up
   - Ink-stained fingers cupping the player's face
   - The marginalia book held open showing the player's name
- **Bloodline-paired with Lyra**: when drawn together, their staffs
  should look made-of-the-same-wood. Lucien's is darker, carved
  with sharper geometry; Lyra's is paler, organic, with shells
  and runes. But the GRAIN should match.

### NOIR (Prince Corvin Noctalis) — Dreamy Seductive (Cavill-Geralt)
- **Palette**: deep violet + black + silver. Gothic but warm-undertoned.
- **Accent**: violet bleed in shadows; silver in highlights;
  occasional scarlet (lips, sealing-wax red).
- **Skin**: pale but warm-undertoned. NOT corpse-pale. He has been
  sealed for 600 years but the player's bonds are warming him.
  A subtle blush sometimes.
- **Hair**: long, jet black, slightly wavy. ALWAYS with one strand
  falling toward the face. Never tied back.
- **Eyes**: deep gold or amber-violet. Heavy-lidded. The most
  hypnotic eyes in the cast.
- **Dreamy tell**: he is ALWAYS looking at the player like he's
  memorizing them. Slow blink. Eyelashes long. The dreamy is in
  the LANGUAGE OF HIS GAZE — heavy, unhurried, never breaking
  contact unless on purpose.
- **Clothing**: gothic prince. High collars. Black velvet, silver
  fastenings, brocade. A signet ring with the Nocthera crest on
  one finger. Cloak when outdoors. NEVER armor.
- **Signature scenes**:
   - The chin-lift: thumb under the player's chin, tilting up
   - The slow wrist-take with eye contact
   - Kneeling at Veyra's grave under the rowan

### PROTO — Golden Retriever Construct (Weaver-avatar)
- **Palette**: prismatic / iridescent rainbow + cyan + soft white.
  Light-based.
- **Accent**: edge-glow that shifts color (cyan to magenta to gold).
- **Skin**: when in his "screen" form, slightly translucent, light
  passing through the edges. When MANIFESTED (high-bond ending),
  skin becomes warm and human-feeling but with prismatic edges
  still visible at the silhouette.
- **Hair**: light blue / silver-violet. ALWAYS slightly animated —
  one strand drifting as if in zero-gravity. He is woven from
  light, after all.
- **Eyes**: cyan or prismatic. Sometimes the iris briefly shows
  data-glyphs (the same way a real eye briefly shows a reflection).
- **Dreamy tell**: when he's not interacting, his form has subtle
  glitch-static at the edges. When he LOOKS at the player, the
  static settles. THAT'S the dreaminess: a being of light who
  becomes solid only because the player is here.
- **Clothing**: soft, asymmetric, modern-fantasy. Looks designed
  but lightly tech-influenced. He has no body to clothe in any
  literal sense — what you draw is what the wardwork has chosen
  to render today.
- **Signature scenes**:
   - Hologram-hand pressed against glass on YOUR side of the screen
   - Sitting cross-legged in his void, face lit by some unseen
     terminal
   - The MANIFEST scene: him in a real body for the first time,
     prismatic edges still showing, hand reaching for the player's

3. SCENE BACKGROUND PALETTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Each character's "home" location has a signature palette. Anytime
you draw a scene IN that location, defer to this palette.

| Character | Location | Palette |
|---|---|---|
| Alistair | Watchhouse / Gate | warm gold + steel grey + torch-orange |
| Elian | Thornwood | forest green + earth brown + moss + pale gold dapple |
| Lyra | Cave / Coast | deep blue + teal + pearl + bioluminescent cyan |
| Caspian | Palace / Balcony | rose-gold + wine + cream + sky violet |
| Lucien | Tower / Study | dusk violet + ink black + candlelight gold + parchment cream |
| Noir | Seal / Nocthera | violet + black + silver + scarlet accent |
| Proto | Void / System | prismatic rainbow + cyan + dark navy + scanline glow |

When two characters share a scene (crossovers), pick a NEUTRAL
palette that isn't either of theirs — usually a midnight blue, a
candle-lit interior, or a forest-edge palette.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. EMOTION FACE-MOOD CHART
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

The game references face emotions: neutral, happy, sad, angry, love,
shy, sleeping, wink, corrupted (and a few others).

Universal rule for ALL characters:
- **neutral**: closed-mouth slight smile or relaxed lips. Eyes soft.
- **happy**: open smile (small for Noir, big for Proto, somewhere
  in between for the rest).
- **sad**: lowered eyelids, downturned mouth corners, brow soft.
- **angry**: NEVER open-mouth shouting. Always controlled. Brow
  down, mouth tight. (Proto is the exception — he doesn't get
  angry at the player. His "angry" face is "alarmed about
  something the player did to themselves.")
- **love**: half-lidded, soft smile, slight blush bloom.
- **shy**: looking away, blush, mouth small or biting lip.
- **wink**: one eye closed, half smile. Caspian and Noir do this
  best; Alistair almost never.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. THINGS TO AVOID
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

- **Hard outlines**. Use thin lines.
- **Identical hair colors across two characters**. Each has a
  distinct dominant hue — don't make Alistair and Caspian both
  brown without differentiation.
- **Modern photo references**. The world is fantasy. Let the
  voice-direction (Heughan, Cavill, Madden) inform mood, not
  photo-realism.
- **Over-rendered armor**. Alistair is the only character who wears
  it, and his is brushed-soft, never mirror-shiny.
- **Sexualized poses for the female character (Lyra)** that the
  male characters wouldn't be drawn in. The whole cast is rendered
  with the same dignity.
- **Stiff symmetry**. Every character should have a slight asymmetry
  in their pose — a hand higher than the other, a hip cocked, a
  head tilt. Never frozen.

6. ASSET DELIVERABLES (per character)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Minimum to ship a character:

  [ ] select-portrait.png  (the character-select grid card)
  [ ] body/neutral.png
  [ ] body/casual1.png
  [ ] body/casual2.png
  [ ] body/happy.png  (or smile.png)
  [ ] body/love.png   (or adoring.png — heart-eyes pose)
  [ ] face/neutral.png
  [ ] face/happy.png
  [ ] face/sad.png
  [ ] face/love.png
  [ ] face/shy.png
  [ ] (character-specific extras: e.g. casting.png for Lucien,
      glitched.png for Proto, calm.png for Elian, etc.)

Plus per-character backgrounds (1–3 each) and any signature props
(staff, cloak, etc.).

Asset path convention (don't break this):
  assets/<charId>/body/<pose>.png
  assets/<charId>/face/<emotion>.png
  assets/bg-<charId>-<location>.png

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. THE FINAL CHECK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Before marking a sprite "done," put it next to a sprite of another
character on screen. If they look like they're in two different
games — go back. Adjust line weight, palette saturation, or skin
gradient until they match.

The art does not have to be technically perfect. It has to feel
like ONE game.
